What it does The procedure becomes the operating system.

One fabric for work, rules, records, and AI execution.

nianav OS treats procedures, records, views, triggers, and connectors as one operating fabric. A procedure is not a document sitting beside the work. It is the work.

Procedures

Work runs inside the approved operating model.

Every step knows its owner, rule, permitted input, review requirement, evidence output, and next action.

Records

Evidence is created while work happens.

Steps can produce records, capture signatures, trigger follow-up, and preserve the audit trail without a separate reconstruction project.

Agents

AI operates under named scope and supervision.

Humans and AI agents can be assigned to steps, but the work stays connected to the procedure, data boundary, custodian, and review path.

Connectors

Specialist tools stay connected.

CAD, clinical systems, finance engines, repositories, and filing tools can remain specialist while nianav OS governs the workflow around them.

Why this matters now Agentic workflow changes the governance problem.

AI is moving from answering questions to executing work.

AI is beginning to read records, use tools, draft outputs, route decisions, and coordinate workflows. For an ordinary company, this is a productivity shift. For a regulated company, it is a governance shift.

Every AI-assisted action must be scoped, explainable, logged, reviewed, and connected to the procedure it belongs to. The question is no longer “can AI do the task?” The question is whether the company can prove what happened, why it happened, who supervised it, what data was used, and whether the result stayed inside the approved operating envelope.

01

The regulated industry gap

Most AI products are built for speed first. Regulated companies need speed, but they also need evidence. Plugging agents into Slack, Notion, SharePoint, or a tracker will not make the audit trail appear later.

02

Startups need this earlier than they think

Evidence starts accumulating from day one, whether the system captures it or not. Scattered procedures, decisions, CAPAs, risks, training, clinical evidence, and AI outputs create audit debt every day.

03

Clinical evidence without the fear

Clinical trials, real-world evidence, validation, and post-market evidence become visible: what requirement is being tested, what supports it, who approved it, what changed, and what still needs follow-up.
