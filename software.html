What nianav OS replaces.

Most of your tools quietly do the same job: they move work between people and keep a record of it. nianav OS replaces that whole layer across quality, operations, product, people, and evidence, including spreadsheets, Notion-style workspaces, and manual coordination, with one connected system.

The boundary What stays outside
Simple systems
Serious impact.
The boundary The work that moves between people comes inside. The specialist tools stay where they are.
01 · It replaces

Tools that just move work around.

  • Tools whose value is routing work through people, roles, records, approvals, evidence, and status.
  • Single-purpose tools that each own one slice of how you operate.
  • Spreadsheets, Notion pages, inboxes, and meetings used as connective tissue between those tools.
02 · It does not replace

Tools that do specialist work.

  • CAD modelling, EDA simulation, git storage, payroll tax calculation, clearance networks, or specialist analytics.
  • Regulators, notified bodies, auditors, lawyers, accountants, or qualified human judgement.
  • Tools whose value is the heavy calculation or the specialist output they produce.
03 · The test

The test is simple.

If the value is who does what, in which order, against which rule, with which evidence, it belongs in nianav OS. If the value is a specialist engine doing specialist work, nianav OS connects to it and governs the workflow around it.

04 · Why it matters

One connected system.

The company stops being a patchwork of tools. The procedures, the records, the view each role sees, and the log of what happened all run from the same place.

What comes inside These look like different tools. Underneath, they are the same thing: steps run over shared records.
Quality

QMS, change control, CAPA, audits, training.

Quality work is not a document library. It is procedures, responsibilities, evidence, reviews, approvals, deviations, and records. All of it lives natively in nianav OS.

Operations

ERP workflow, procurement, supplier qualification, inventory.

The purchasing manager can feel like they are using an ERP, but the underlying step still lives once in the shared record of what happened, with the records it touches.

Product

Design review, release approval, engineering changes.

nianav OS does not draw the CAD model or run the simulation. It owns the review, sign-off, change record, release evidence, and downstream carry-through.

People

HRIS workflow, onboarding, capability, training, handover.

People records, roles, capabilities, assignments, training debt, and handover are part of the operating system because they determine who can perform which step.

Customer & IT

Ticketing, support, service management, escalations.

A ticket is a piece of tracked work with an owner, a deadline, evidence, comments, escalations, and a clear close. It is structured work, not another isolated queue.

Finance & admin

Approvals, period close, payroll workflow, submissions.

The workflow comes inside. The specialist calculation or filing engine stays connected. The system knows what was approved, by whom, against which requirement, and where the external output lives.

What stays outside Not because it does not matter. Because it does specialist work, not workflow.

Specialist tools remain specialist.

CAD, EDA, modelling, simulation.nianav OS governs design review, release approval, ECOs, evidence, and carry-through. The specialist modelling environment remains the source for the technical artifact.
Git, code hosting, developer tooling.Code review, release authorisation, deployment approval, and audit evidence can run in nianav OS. The git engine, repository, diff rendering, and code search stay specialist.
Payroll, tax, e-invoicing, statutory accounting.Approval, exception review, evidence archive, and connector governance come inside. Gross-to-net math, statutory accounts, tax advice, and clearance protocols stay with specialist providers.
Regulators, auditors, notified bodies, legal advice.nianav OS organises readiness, evidence, findings, CAPA, and traceability. It does not replace the authority or judgement of external bodies and qualified advisors.
What that means One source of truth, shown the way each role expects.

The same work can look like a different tool to each person.

A quality officer can feel like they are using a quality system. A purchaser can feel like they are using an ERP. A production worker can feel like they are using a shop-floor system. An HR manager can feel like they are using an HR platform.

The point is that the work exists only once. The same record, owner, evidence, due date, and history show up differently for each role, without becoming separate copies in separate systems.

That is how nianav OS lets you simply ask the company a question. What is blocked, who owns it, which rule applies, what evidence exists, what changed, and what happens next. No piecing the truth back together from a dozen tools.

Evidence Clinical evidence becomes visible work.

Clinical evidence without the fear.

Clinical trials, real-world evidence, design validation, and post-market evidence are how a regulated company shows that its product does what it claims, for the people it is intended to serve, under the conditions it will actually face.

nianav OS makes that evidence visible: what requirement is being tested, what evidence supports it, what record was created, who approved it, what changed, and what still needs follow-up.

Agentic governance When AI does the work, it still needs an owner and a record.

When AI does the work, it needs clear limits and a named owner.

In regulated work, an AI agent is not a chatbot. It does real work, so it needs the same things a person would. A named version, an approved task, clear limits, a human who owns the result, the data it was allowed to touch, and a sealed record of what it read and wrote.

That is the difference between "we used AI" and "we can defend how AI was used." nianav OS lets startups use agentic workflows without losing control of evidence, review, or accountability.

Standards & regulation You turn on the frameworks that apply to you. The rest stays off.

Regulated or not, the workflow shape is the same.

Quality systems

ISO 9001, ISO 13485, GxP and GMP logic

Controlled processes, role-based responsibilities, training awareness, deviations, CAPA, change control, approval trails, and evidence capture.

Product & medical

EU MDR, IVDR, FDA QMSR and 21 CFR Part 11

Design controls, traceability, electronic records, review gates, sign-off logic, and the link between work performed and evidence retained.

Security & governance

ISO 27001, GDPR and SOC 2-ready controls

Access control, ownership, auditability, data boundaries, policy-to-work mapping, and operational proof for governance reviews.

Important: nianav OS should not be read as a legal, regulatory, notified-body, or auditor replacement. It maps obligations into executable workflow, captures evidence, and keeps the system current around your declared scope. The responsibility for compliance remains with the company and its qualified advisors.
