S. 01 · Agentic governance AI execution belongs inside procedure, not beside it.

Agentic workflow needs a controlled operating envelope.

In regulated work, an AI agent is not a chatbot. It is an executor inside a controlled operating model. It needs a named version, a validated capability, a permitted scope, a human principal, a custodian, an input contract, a reasoning or execution trace, and a sealed record of what it read and wrote.

That is the difference between “we used AI” and “we can defend how AI was used.” nianav OS lets startups use agentic workflows without losing control of evidence, review, or accountability.

VersionWhich agent or capability ran.
ScopeWhat it was allowed to do.
InputWhich record or data boundary it used.
ReviewWho supervised and approved the result.
RecordWhat was sealed into the audit trail.
S. 02 · The boundary Workflow-shaped work comes inside. Specialist engines stay connected.
01

It replaces workflow-shaped tools.

  • Tools whose value is routing work through people, roles, records, approvals, evidence, and status.
  • Point solutions that each own one slice of operational reality.
  • Spreadsheets, inboxes, and meetings used as connective tissue between those tools.
02

It does not replace specialist engines.

  • CAD modelling, EDA simulation, git storage, payroll tax calculation, clearance networks, or specialist analytics.
  • Regulators, notified bodies, auditors, lawyers, accountants, or qualified human judgement.
  • Specialist systems whose value is a calculation engine, rendering surface, protocol layer, or domain-specific physics.
03

The test is simple.

If the value is who does what, in which order, against which rule, with which evidence, it belongs in nianav OS. If the value is a specialist engine doing specialist work, nianav OS connects to it and governs the workflow around it.

04

Why that matters.

The company stops being a patchwork of tools and becomes one operating fabric: procedures, records, role-based views, triggers, connectors, capabilities, and step execution logs all working from the same substrate.

S. 03 · What comes inside The categories look different. Underneath, they are procedure over records.
Quality

QMS, change control, CAPA, audits, training.

Quality work is not a document library. It is procedures, responsibilities, evidence, reviews, approvals, deviations, records, and recurring proof. That shape is native to nianav OS.

Operations

ERP workflow, procurement, supplier qualification, inventory movement.

The purchasing manager can feel like they are using an ERP surface, but the underlying step still lives once in the canonical execution log with the records it touches.

Product

PLM workflow, design review, release approval, engineering change.

nianav OS does not draw the CAD model or run the simulation. It owns the review, sign-off, change record, release evidence, and downstream carry-through.

People

HRIS workflow, onboarding, capability, training, handover.

People records, roles, capabilities, assignments, training debt, and handover are part of the operating system because they determine who can perform which step.

Customer and IT

Ticketing, support, service management, escalations.

A ticket is a procedure instance with owners, deadlines, evidence, comments, escalations, and closure conditions. nianav OS turns that into structured work instead of another isolated queue.

Finance and admin

Approvals, period close, payroll workflow, expenses, regulatory submissions.

The workflow comes inside. The specialist calculation or filing engine stays connected. The system knows what was approved, by whom, against which requirement, and where the external output lives.

S. 04 · What stays outside Not because it is unimportant, but because it is not workflow-shaped.

Specialist tools remain specialist.

CAD, EDA, modelling, simulation. nianav OS governs design review, release approval, ECOs, evidence, and carry-through. The specialist modelling or simulation environment remains the source for the technical artifact.
Git, code hosting, developer tooling. Code review, release authorisation, deployment approval, and audit evidence can run in nianav OS. The git engine, repository, diff rendering, and code search remain in specialist tools.
Payroll, tax, e-invoicing, statutory accounting. Approval, exception review, evidence archive, and connector governance come inside. Gross-to-net math, statutory accounts production, tax position advice, and clearance protocols stay with specialist providers.
Regulators, auditors, notified bodies, legal advice. nianav OS can organise readiness, evidence, findings, CAPA, and traceability. It does not replace the authority or judgement of external bodies and qualified advisors.
S. 05 · What that means One substrate, many role-native surfaces.

The same work can look like a different tool to each person.

A QA officer can feel like they are using a QMS. A purchaser can feel like they are using an ERP. A production worker can feel like they are using an MES. An HR manager can feel like they are using an HRIS.

The point is that the step lives once. The same canonical record, same owner, same evidence, same due date, same execution log, and same audit trail can render differently by persona without becoming different data in different systems.

That is how nianav OS makes the company queryable. You can ask what is blocked, who owns it, which rule applies, which evidence exists, what changed, and what needs to happen next without reconstructing the truth from a fragmented stack.

S. 06 · Evidence Clinical evidence becomes visible work.

Clinical evidence without the fear.

Clinical trials, real-world evidence, design validation, and post-market evidence are how a regulated company shows that its product does what it claims, for the people it is intended to serve, under the conditions it will actually face.

nianav OS makes that evidence visible. What requirement is being tested, what evidence supports it, what record was created, who approved it, what changed, and what still needs follow-up.

Requirement under test The system connects evidence work to the claim, risk, procedure, or intended-use requirement it supports.
Record created Work produces the record while it happens, instead of asking the team to reconstruct it before a review or audit.
Follow-up controlled Changes, exceptions, CAPAs, and approvals stay attached to the same operating record.
S. 07 · Standards and regulation Compliance machinery activates per tenant, based on declared scope.

Regulated or not, the workflow shape is the same.

nianav OS can serve regulated and non-regulated process-driven companies. For regulated tenants, the compliance machinery activates through the tenant's declared regulatory scope. For non-regulated tenants, the same workflow rigour remains, without claiming framework coverage.

Quality systems

ISO 9001, ISO 13485, GxP and GMP logic

Controlled processes, role-based responsibilities, training awareness, deviations, CAPA, change control, approval trails, and evidence capture.

Product and medical

EU MDR, IVDR, FDA QMSR and 21 CFR Part 11 direction

Design controls, traceability, electronic records, review gates, sign-off logic, and the link between work performed and evidence retained.

Security and governance

ISO 27001, GDPR and SOC 2-ready controls

Access control, ownership, auditability, data boundaries, policy-to-work mapping, and operational proof for governance reviews.

Important: nianav OS should not be read as legal, regulatory, notified-body, or auditor replacement. It maps obligations into executable workflow, captures evidence, and keeps the system current around your declared scope. The responsibility for compliance remains with the company and its qualified advisors.
